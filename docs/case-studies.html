PRIVACY & SECURITY INNOVATION PROGRAM DESIGN

Security Innovation

Unlike software that simply de-identifies data, KI Data Anonymized Analytics converts identifying data into analytics, allowing you to transfer insights instead of identities. This high-performance data security platform can help you to move confidently and quickly towards better data sharing with minimal privacy risk.

KI Anonymized Analytics offers solutions for both structured and unstructured data spaces. Rather than producing de-identified data, the solution provides and responds to analytics queries. It offers the insights of data analytics without revealing personal information. This solution enables our clients to transfer information across boundaries, including those between agency departments or between agencies.

KI Anonymized Analytics also enables secondary data use. It gives secondary-use analysts access to the insights they need without giving access to the data itself. KI Data Anonymized Analytics offers a management dashboard which allows the data custodian to control all aspects of secondary use. At the same time, our processes reduce the risk of unintended disclosure, keep you connected and empower you to make the most of your data across environments.

Project Objective

Develop a legislative report analyzing policies, concerns, and relevant practical barriers pertaining to new applications supporting patient access to personal health information

Approach

KI Design supported an innovation hub in the development of a privacy and security program to facilitate a local health research network’s collaborative programs.

We offered our tailored information, access, and privacy services following the Privacy in Design method, focusing on issues most relevant to the organization. We provided privacy expertise, recommendations, and practical implementation tools to resolve privacy concerns and highlight relevant issues at the nexus of policy and privacy.

Our final report included a review identifying privacy, security, and access concerns for Ontario patients, including those that arose from legal or policy barriers, and those stemming from other practical barriers

Methods

  • Identified privacy, security and access concerns for citizens of Ontario
  • Identified legal and policy-based barriers to access or sources of privacy concern
  • Identified physical and practical barriers to access or sources of privacy concern
  • Reviewed practices in other jurisdictions
  • Identified concrete solutions to other privacy concerns

Highlights

  • Reviewed existing practices
  • Identified legal and policy barriers to information access
  • Identified physical and practical barriers to information access
  • Offered a framework explaining key barriers preventing patients from accessing their own personal health information
